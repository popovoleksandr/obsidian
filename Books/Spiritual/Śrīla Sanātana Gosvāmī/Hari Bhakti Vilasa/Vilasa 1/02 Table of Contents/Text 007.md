prātaḥ smṛtyādi kṛṣṇasya 
vādyādyaiś ca prabodhanam
nirmālyottaraṇādy-ādau 
maṅgalārātrikaṁ tataḥ

prātaḥ—at sunrise; smṛty—remembering; ādi—beginning with; kṛṣṇasya—of Lord Kṛṣṇa; vādya-ādyaiḥ—activities beginning with playing music; ca—and; prabodhanam—awakening; nirmālya—the garlands worn by the Lord; uttaraṇa —wearing; ādy—beginning with; ādau—in the beginning; maṅgalārātrikam—maṅgala-ārati; tataḥ—then.

. . . (Third Vilāsa) 13. remembering Lord Kṛṣṇa and performing other devotional activities early in the morning, 14. awakening the Deity with music and other devotional activities, 15. wearing garlands worn by the Deity and accepting other remnants from the Deity, 16. maṅala-ārati, . . .
